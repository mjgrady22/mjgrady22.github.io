Mount Vesuvius

The Eruption

  • Mount Vesuvius erupted on August 24th of 79 A.D.
  • Molten rock and ash shot 12 miles into the sky
  • When the material cooled, it was a cement like substance
  • 2,000 people were killed in the eruption
  • At the time of the eruption, there were 20,000 people living in Pompeii
  • There were survivors of the eruption, because there was time to evacuate
  • The eruption covered the region in a layer of ash 16 feet deep
  • The 1,300 degree lava traveled at 200 mph, covering 200 square miles

The Volcano

  • It hasn't erupted since 1944
  • It is currently overdue for an eruption
  • It has erupted more than 50 times
  • It is still considered one of the most dangerous volcanoes in the world
  • It is one of several volcanoes which form the Campanian volcanic arc
